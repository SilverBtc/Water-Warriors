Water-Saving Tips

Simple actions that make a big difference

Be a Water Warrior in Your Daily Life

Every drop counts! Small changes in your daily habits can save thousands of liters of water each year. Explore our tips and DIY ideas to reduce your water footprint and help protect this precious resource.

Bathroom Water-Saving Tips

Take Shorter Showers

A 10-minute shower can use up to 25 liters of water. Try to limit showers to 5 minutes or less.

Potential savings: 10+ liters per shower

Turn Off the Tap

Don't let the water run while brushing teeth, shaving, or washing your face. This simple habit can save significant amounts of water.

Potential savings: 8 liters per day

Check for Toilet Leaks

Add a few drops of food coloring to your toilet tank. If color appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak that should be fixed.

Potential savings: Up to 200 liters per day

Install Low-Flow Fixtures

Install water-efficient showerheads, faucet aerators, and toilets to significantly reduce your water usage without sacrificing performance.

Potential savings: Up to 60% per fixture

Kitchen Water-Saving Tips

Run Full Loads Only

Only run your dishwasher when it's full. This maximizes water efficiency and reduces the total number of cycles needed.

Potential savings: Up to 320 liters per month

Washing Dishes by Hand

If washing dishes by hand, don't let the water run. Fill one basin with wash water and the other with rinse water.

Potential savings: 10 liters per dishwashing session

Thaw Food in the Refrigerator

Don't thaw frozen foods under running water. Plan ahead and thaw in the refrigerator overnight.

Potential savings: Up to 5 liters per thawing session

Use a Basin for Washing Produce

Instead of washing fruits and vegetables under running water, fill a bowl with water and wash them there.

Potential savings: 2-3 liters per meal preparation

Laundry Water-Saving Tips

Wait for Full Loads

Washing full loads of laundry is more water-efficient than washing several small loads.

Potential savings: 15-45 liters per load

Adjust Water Levels

If your washer has adjustable water level settings, always match the water level to the size of your load.

Potential savings: Up to 30 liters per load

Choose Water-Efficient Appliances

When it's time to replace your washer, choose an ENERGY STAR certified model, which can use up to 50% less water than standard washers.

Potential savings: Up to 7,000 liters per year

Wash in Cold Water

Using cold water for washing saves energy and is often just as effective as using hot water, especially with modern detergents.

Energy savings: Up to 90% of washing energy

Outdoor Water-Saving Tips

Water During Cooler Hours

Water your garden and lawn in the early morning or evening when temperatures are cooler to minimize evaporation.

Potential savings: Up to 30% of irrigation water

Choose Native Plants

Plant drought-resistant, native plants in your garden. They require less water and maintenance once established.

Potential savings: Up to 50-75% of landscape water

Collect Rainwater

Use rain barrels to collect water from your roof for watering plants and gardens.

Potential collection: Up to 1,300 liters during rainy season

Use a Broom, Not a Hose

Clean driveways and sidewalks with a broom instead of hosing them down.

Potential savings: Up to 150 liters each time

DIY Water-Saving Projects

DIY Rain Barrel

Collect rainwater for garden use with this simple DIY project:

  1. Get a food-grade plastic barrel (200-litters)
  2. Cut a hole in the lid for downspout water intake
  3. Install a spigot near the bottom for water access
  4. Add an overflow pipe near the top
  5. Place on elevated platform under a downspout

Potential water savings: Hundreds of liters during rainy season!

Greywater Recycling System

Reuse shower and sink water for toilet flushing or garden irrigation:

  1. Redirect shower/sink drain pipes to collection container
  2. Install a simple filtration system (for solid particles)
  3. Add a small pump if gravity flow isn't sufficient
  4. Connect to toilet tank or outdoor irrigation

Note: Check local regulations before implementing.

Potential water savings: Up to 50-80 liters per day for a family of four!

Toilet Water Displacement

Reduce water used per flush with this 5-minute project:

  1. Fill a plastic bottle with water and small rocks (for weight)
  2. Seal it tightly
  3. Place inside toilet tank away from operating mechanisms

Caution: Don't use a brick as it can deteriorate and damage plumbing.

Potential water savings: 0.5-1 liter per flush!
