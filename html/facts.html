Water Facts

Discover the reality of water scarcity around the world

Understanding Water Scarcity

Water scarcity affects every continent and was listed in 2019 by the World Economic Forum as one of the largest global risks in terms of potential impact over the next decade. It's an issue that affects billions of people worldwide and requires immediate attention and action.

Did you know?

By 2025, half of the world's population will be living in water-stressed areas.

The Global Water Crisis

  • 785 million people lack basic drinking water access, that's 1 in 9 people worldwide
  • Women and girls spend an estimated 200 million hours hauling water every day
  • The average American uses 80-100 liters of water per day
  • More than 2 billion people live in countries experiencing high water stress
  • Around 4 billion people experience severe water scarcity during at least one month of the year

Water Pollution

  • 80% of wastewater is released to the environment without adequate treatment
  • Around 1.8 billion people use a source of drinking water contaminated with feces
  • Every day, nearly 1,000 children die due to preventable water and sanitation-related diseases
  • Unsafe water kills more people each year than all forms of violence, including war
  • Agriculture accounts for 70% of global freshwater withdrawals, mainly for irrigation

Climate Change and Water

  • Rising temperatures are changing precipitation patterns, causing droughts and floods
  • By 2030, water scarcity in some arid regions will displace up to 700 million people
  • Climate change causes more frequent and intense storms, leading to increased runoff and reduced water quality
  • Melting glaciers threaten the water supply of millions who depend on seasonal melt water
  • Rising sea levels cause saltwater intrusion into freshwater aquifers in coastal areas
